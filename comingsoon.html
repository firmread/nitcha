COMING SOON

 

Interactions

 

The first state of interaction is if the person wearing the key body sits in bad posture. The motion of leaning forward causes the shirt to disrupt the wearer’s bad habit by sending messages to the computer.

 

The second state is if the person keeps sitting in the bad posture, the shirt will continue to send messages until the wearable keyboard function is triggered. This allows the person to type on the computer screen by typing on the surface of the shirt, introducing the element of physical feedback. Instead of pressing a keyboard, what if you pressed your body to type? This requires more movements, and means that to type words we must also remember a set of physical gestures. Ironically, this interaction functions like punishment and healing at the same time.
